From District to Nationals: My IndiaSkills Cybersecurity Story

Published on July 2, 2024

Every journey begins with a single step — and mine began at NIELIT Patna, where I first discovered the opportunity to participate in the IndiaSkills competition, a prestigious platform that brings together the best young talent across various vocational trades in India.

The Beginning: Online Round

I chose the Cybersecurity trade, a field I’ve always been passionate about. The initial round was conducted online and tested participants on soft skills, aptitude, and cybersecurity knowledge. When I cleared this round, it felt like my first big leap toward something greater.

The Track 2 Route: Going Beyond Limits

Since my state didn’t sponsor IndiaSkills candidates, I took the Track 2 path, organized by SSC NASSCOM. This alternate route included:

  • Round 1: An AI-proctored test covering system hardening, malware analysis, and competition safety.
  • Round 2: A 2-day competition at Ethnus, Bangalore featuring intense practical rounds in server hardening, vulnerability analysis, and log monitoring.

The Nationals: Delhi

Being selected for the IndiaSkills Nationals in Delhi was a dream come true. It spanned five days, starting with a grand opening ceremony, followed by three days of long, demanding practicals in areas like:

  • Linux & Windows Server hardening
  • Apache & IIS configuration
  • Splunk & SIEM operations
  • Capture the Flag (CTF) challenges

Unfortunately, technical issues disrupted the first two days — including firewall problems and VM crashes. Despite those setbacks, I pushed through, even during the final 12-hour challenge.

Lessons Beyond the Scoreboard

Although I didn’t win a medal, the experience gave me much more: confidence, friendships, a certificate of participation, and a deeper understanding of real-world cybersecurity under pressure.

This journey taught me that competitions aren’t just about winning — they’re about learning, adapting, and evolving. I’m grateful for the opportunity, and excited for what lies ahead.

Thank you to NIELIT, SSC NASSCOM, and every peer who stood shoulder-to-shoulder with me on this incredible path.
